## Abstract

This thesis investigates innovative technologies and strategies for mitigating bird strike and drone strike risks in the aeronautical industry.  
The work analyzes international regulations, vulnerable aircraft components, advanced materials, numerical simulations, experimental impact tests, airport environmental control strategies, and emerging prevention systems based on radar, artificial intelligence, autonomous detection, and anti-drone technologies.

The study highlights the importance of combining structural protection, advanced materials, predictive monitoring, and coordinated risk-management procedures to improve flight safety and reduce the operational and economic impact of bird and drone strike events.
